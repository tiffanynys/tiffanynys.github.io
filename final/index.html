    The plight of sexual violence victims in Hong Kong

Sexual Violence in Hong Kong

#MeToo movement is an attempt to demonstrate the widespread prevalence of sexual assault and harassment. The waves of this movement started with a hashtag on social media in 2017. Ever since, more and more women came forward on social media with their shared experiences of sexual harassment.

Sexual Violence

What is sexual violence?

Sexual violence refers to the intended offence in action, language or attitude with sexual implications, which make a person feel uncomfortable.

By this definition, sexual violence includes not only to rape and indecent assault, but also sexual abuse without body contact. Examples are sexual harassment, dirty jokes, and sex-oriented comment.

Sexual violence has nothing to do with the relationship or gender of involved parties. Both male and female can be victims of sexual violence.


Situations in Hong Kong

According to RainLily, Hong Kong’s first sexual violence crisis centre launched in 2000, the situation of female victims is worrying.


Increasing number of reported cases of sexual abuse

The number of cases of ‘Rape’, ‘Indecent assault’ and ‘Sexual harassment’ has gone up. Over the past 18 years, the number of ‘Rape’ cases increased from 128 to 446, which is three times the original.

Reported cases by district

According to the Social Welfare Department, ‘Yau Tsim Mong’ and ‘Central and Western’ are the two districts with the most reported sexual violence cases last year.




Most of the perpetrators are acquaintances of the victims

RainLily also found that most perpetrators and victims are acquaintances. Around 80% of victims know the perpetrator prior to the incident, whereas strangers only take up 20% of the perpetrators.

Teenage girls tend to have longer delay reporting time

RainLily discovered that female victims have serious and common delay reporting phenomenon among sexual violence victims. A relationship between the length of delay reporting time and the victim’s age exist. On average, females above 16 reported their cases 444 days after the incident, while females under 16 delayed 4814 days.

Misconceptions of sexual violations
  • Concensual sex: two-thirds of the respondents mistakenly believe that consensual sex is a form of sexual violation
  • Uncomfortable about being asked for the size of breasts: only 55 out of 99 respondents noticed that this is a form of sexual violation despite there is no phsyical contact.
  • Forced sex by partner: around 10% of the respondents did not notice that forced sex of their partners can be a form of sexual violence
  • Being winked at by a stranger: This is not a form of sexual violence, but 11 of the respondents did not believe so


    • The awareness of sexual assaults

    Hong Kong young female adults are aware of different forms of sexual violations. 77 of the respondents have scored from 6 to 8 out of 8. Around one-third got 7 out of 8. Those whose scores are from 1-5 out of 8 only takes up a very small percentage of the respondents. However, only 13 female got all the correct answers. There are still rooms of improvement in sex education.

    Reaction towards sexual violence

    How will females respond to sexual violence?

    Higher tolerance for acquaintances

    The respondents may not report their cases to the police if they are sexually violated. Only 18% of the respondents will report to the police if they are sexually violated. The majority, 81% of the respondents, indicates that other factors may affect their decision on whether to report to the police. Among those who consider other factors, if they are sexually violated by someone they know, only half of them choose to report to the police.

    Sex education in Hong Kong

    The ideal VS reality

    Age of receiving sexual education

    Most of the female respondents wish that they received sex education earlier. Most of the respondents received sex education between the age of 6 to 12 and 13 to 15. However, nearly 70% of all respondents wish that sex education was provided when they were under six years old.
